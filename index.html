The Golden Age of
Board Games

Gone are the days when "board games" just meant Monopoly or Scrabble. In this story, we'll explore how games have evolved in complexity over time -- and help you find your next obsession.

By Mo & Angela

1. The Era of Luck

2. The Strategic Turn

In the late 90s, Catan was published. Its popularity indicated that consumers were willing to buy more than just

Mechanics like Hand Management (playing cards from your hand at the right time) gave players control. Games like Catan proved that you could trade, build, and negotiate your way to victory.

3. An Explosion of Ideas

In the past twenty years, with the rise of the Internet, board games have exploded in popularity. We saw the rise of Worker Placement (drafting actions), Deck Building (constructing your own deck as you play), and Cooperative Games (winning or losing together).

Games became diverse ecosystems of mechanics.

1. Defining "Weight"

BoardGameGeek uses a metric called Weight (1-5) to measure complexity.

1.0 - 2.0: Party games like Uno or Codenames. Simple rules, quick fun.
3.0+: Strategy games like Scythe or Terraforming Mars. Complex rules, deep planning.
