The Golden Age of
Board Games

How design trends have evolved from simple roll-and-moves to complex strategic masterpieces.

By Mo & Angela

The Early Days

For decades, board games were defined by luck. Roll a die, move a pawn. Think Monopoly or Sorry!. But starting in the late 90s, something changed.

The Eurogame Revolution

Designers began prioritizing strategy over luck. Mechanics like Worker Placement and Resource Management took center stage.

Complexity Rising

As the audience grew more sophisticated, so did the games. The average "weight" (complexity) of top-rated games has steadily climbed.

Modern Hybrids

Today, the best games blend rich themes with tight mechanics. Let's explore what makes a modern classic.

The Complexity Creep

In the 90s, popular games were simple. The average complexity (weight) was around 2.0.

Heavier is Better?

Interestingly, there is a strong correlation between complexity and user rating. "Heavier" games tend to be rated higher by the BGG community.

The Sweet Spot

However, the very heaviest games (weight > 4.5) see a drop-off. The "sweet spot" seems to be around 3.5 - 4.0.
